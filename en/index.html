Profile

About

As the person responsible for NEC's environmental management strategy, Yutaka leads the company's cross-functional initiatives spanning SBT 1.5°C, RE100, TCFD, and TNFD. He is particularly focused on Supply Chain CO2 (Scope 3 & product CFP), leading international rule-making efforts while developing commercial solutions based on NEC's own implementation experience.

In the field of nature capital and biodiversity, Yutaka serves as an International Committee Member of the TNFD Data Catalyst and helped shape enterprise disclosure frameworks. In July 2023, he led the creation of the first TNFD report from Japan's IT industry — a landmark achievement followed by a 2nd (2024) and 3rd (2025) edition. He has supported TNFD disclosure adoption across a wide range of industries including apparel, food & beverage, housing, equipment manufacturers, and finance.

Prior to NEC, Yutaka spent 14 years at Toyota Motor Corporation (including 9 years in North America), overseeing environmental strategies for 60 plants across 30 countries. He then served as an Executive Officer at A-Zero Co., Ltd., where he launched the "Mori no Unagi" (Forest Eel) circular agriculture-forestry-fishery brand in Nishiawakura Village, Okayama — realizing a world-first circular economic model and achieving profitability.

Yutaka is currently also exploring the application of LLM and Agentic-AI to enterprise environmental risk minimization and opportunity maximization. His driving belief, shaped by witnessing environmental degradation during his upbringing in Odawara, is that "businesses can generate profit while enriching nature."
