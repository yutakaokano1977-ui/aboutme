ENVIRONMENTAL STRATEGIST

Realizing Coexistence
of Nature and Economy
Through Business.

An ecologist by training, Yutaka Okano has led environmental transformation at Toyota and NEC. Based in Nishiawakura, Okayama, he builds business models where natural capital and economic value reinforce each other. He continues to share Japan's insights on the global stage at forums including G7 and CBD-COP16.

CAREER

Career

2022 — Present

NEC Corporation

Director, Supply Chain Sustainability Management Division

Driving corporate sustainability transformation through renewable energy adoption, data center water resource management, and TNFD report development. Representing NEC's initiatives at international forums including G7 and CBD-COP16.

2017 — 2022

A-Zero Inc.

Executive Officer (Nishiawakura Village, Okayama)

Founded a natural capital business unit leveraging the village's forestry-agriculture circulation system. Built profitable-yet-nature-positive business models including "Mori no Unagi," a sustainable eel farm in a repurposed schoolhouse.

2003 — 2017

Toyota Motor Corporation

Environmental Division (14 years)

Led global environmental initiatives spanning decarbonization, resource circulation, water conservation, and biodiversity. Drove environmental policy across domestic and international sites and participated in international negotiations.
